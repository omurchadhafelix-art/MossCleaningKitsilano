The Real Problem

Moss looks harmless.
It isn't.

Kitsilano's mature trees, north-facing slopes, and coastal moisture create near-perfect conditions for moss to establish fast and spread deeper into your shingles each year.

  • 01Moss can lift shingles. Root systems can grow beneath shingle edges, potentially creating gaps that allow water to reach underlying materials. Oregon State University Extension research confirms this in Pacific Northwest roofs.
  • 02It holds moisture continuously. Moss traps water against your shingles around the clock. On cedar shake, which is common throughout Kitsilano, this can accelerate rot significantly over time.
  • 03Insurance considerations. BC insurers generally expect homeowners to maintain their roofs proactively. Documented neglect can complicate future moss-related claims.
  • 04Flagged during inspections. Roof moss is routinely noted in home inspections and can affect negotiations in Vancouver's property market.
On damage claims: The research cited comes from industry professionals and academic sources. Results vary by roof type, age, climate exposure, and moss coverage. We always recommend a professional assessment of your specific roof.

How much does moss removal cost in Kitsilano?
Our prices start from $399. Metro Vancouver roof cleaning typically ranges from $300 to $900, with severe infestations across BC reaching up to $1,500. Kitsilano homes generally fall between $450 and $900 depending on roof size and moss coverage. All quotes are free and come with no obligation.
Source: Shine City Pressure Washing, 2026 · ClearView Home Services, BC, 2025
How often does roof moss need treating in Vancouver?
BC roofing professionals generally recommend treatment every 2.5 to 4 years. Homes with heavy tree canopy or north-facing slopes may benefit from treatment closer to every 2 years. Applying an inhibitor after removal significantly slows regrowth.
Source: ClearView Home Services · BC roofing industry standard
Can moss actually damage my roof?
Research from Oregon State University Extension indicates moss can lift shingles, potentially allowing water to reach underlying roof materials. BC roofing professionals suggest significant untreated moss growth may contribute to reduced roof lifespan. Individual outcomes depend on roof type, age, and moss coverage, so we recommend a professional assessment of your specific situation.
Source: Oregon State University Extension · Shine-Pros.ca, BC, 2025
Is the treatment safe for cedar shake roofs?
Yes. Low-pressure chemical treatment is widely considered the most appropriate method for cedar shake. High-pressure washing can strip natural oils and fibres from cedar, potentially shortening roof life. Our biodegradable solutions are safe for asphalt, cedar shake, and tile roofs.
Do I need to be home during treatment?
No. We need safe access to your roof and an outdoor water tap. You receive full written documentation on completion and can pay by e-transfer or credit card.
What areas do you serve?
Primarily Kitsilano, Point Grey, Dunbar and Kerrisdale. We also take select jobs across Fairview and Vancouver's west side. Get in touch to confirm your address is within our area.
